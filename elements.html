Real Estate Acquisition Price Model

Utilized Excel and Mathematics

Using mathetmatics, this real estate offer price model was built in Excel. By calculating and analzying different aspects, such as cash flow, cash-on-cash return, NOI, cap rate, and mortgage, it benefits investors due to is speed and accuracy in calculating correct offer prices, evaluating deals, and ultimately acquiring project funding.

Monthly Mortgage Payment = P[r(1+r)^n/((1+r)^n)-1]

  • P = Principal Amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate
  • n = Number of months you will have to repay your loan for

Loan Amount = (PMT/i)*[1-1/(1+i)^n]

  • PMT = Monthly payment
  • i = Interest rate per month in decimal form (interest rate percentage divided by 12)
  • n = Number of months (term of the loan in months)

Cap Rate = Net Operating Income/Offer Price
Cap Rate is a measure of investment return. A higher cap rate implies a higher potential return but may indicate higher risks. The ideal cap rate is around 5% to 10%.

Return on Investment(ROI) = (Net income/Cost of investment)*100%
ROI is a financial metric which measures the profitability and efficiency of an investment. A high ROI indicates a profitable investment where returns exceed the investment costs. The higher the percentage, the more favorable the investment is considered. The minimum request was 10%

Debt Coverage Ratio(DCR) = Net Operating Income/Annual Debt Service
DCR is generally assessed by money lenders to evaluate the ability of a property to generate enough income to cover its debt obligations. A higher DCR indicates a more favorable financial position and lower risk for lenders. Lenders often use 1.25 as the minimum DCR requirement.
